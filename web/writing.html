ElevenAgents for enterprise workflows

Voice as the front door for every workflow on your enterprise platform.

Most voice agent deployments today stop at the contact center. The agent picks up the phone, answers a question, sometimes books a callback. That's a real market and ElevenLabs is winning it: Klarna runs front-line US phone support for roughly 35 million customers and resolves issues 10x faster at 90% lower cost per resolution. Revolut handles tier-one support in 31 languages from a single configuration. Better.com doubled lead-to-lock conversion while cutting origination cost by 41 percent.

These are the headline customers. They define the category. They also define its current ceiling.

The next leg of enterprise voice-agent adoption is inside the systems of record where the work actually gets done. That's where the real friction lives, and where ElevenAgents has its strongest unclaimed position.

Why now

Enterprise IT buyers spent the last eighteen months consolidating around the platforms they already operate. Vendor sprawl is out. Platform leverage is in. The four enterprise platforms that show up in a CIO budget meeting — Salesforce, ServiceNow, Zendesk, HubSpot — are all on ElevenLabs' integrations page today. The plumbing is built. What hasn't been built yet is the GTM narrative that lets a ServiceNow platform owner or a Salesforce admin see ElevenAgents as something they can pull into their roadmap on Monday.

That's a positioning gap, not a product gap. And positioning gaps are the cheapest growth lever a category leader has.

Three moves this unlocks

Customer-facing agents that act in the system of record. Today's voice agents mostly talk; the better ones look things up. The best ones close tickets, update opportunities, file incidents, and trigger workflows. When the agent is wired into ServiceNow, “I lost my badge” doesn't just route to a human — it opens the right incident, attaches the right knowledge article, and runs the approval flow. The customer experience is faster. The downstream work is already done.

Internal-facing agents. This is the underbought market. IT helpdesk on the night shift. HR benefits questions during open enrollment. Sales coaching the night before a renewal. Compliance Q&A buried inside a policy document nobody has time to read. Internal employees are a captive audience with a much shorter security review and a faster ROI calculation than any external deployment. ElevenAgents has the latency, the multilingual range, and the platform integrations to own this category. The story to tell it hasn't been told.

Compound agents that hand off with context. The interesting architecture isn't one agent. It's an external agent that escalates to an internal one without losing the thread — the customer's incident, the rep's call notes, the platform's CRM record, all available to the next voice in the chain. That's a workflow only a platform-aware voice agent can deliver, and it's the kind of demo that closes platform-owner deals.

What's in production today

The customers I'd point a ServiceNow CIO to today are external-facing. Klarna's 10x faster resolution at 90% lower cost across roughly 35 million US customers. Revolut's 8x resolution time across 31 languages from one configuration. Better.com's 2x conversion at 41% lower origination cost. These prove the layer works at scale, under load, with multilingual coverage, inside regulated industries. The compliance posture is already there — SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI DSS Level 1, GDPR, TXRAMP Level 2, Zero Retention mode, and regional data residency across the US, EU, and India. That's the current state, not a future readiness story.

The internal-facing parallel — IT helpdesk, HR, sales coaching, compliance Q&A — is the next chapter, and the customer logos there are a positioning gap waiting to be filled.

Voice as the front door

Every form, every portal, every ticket-routing menu, every half-typed Slack message that should have been a question lives inside a platform of record. ElevenAgents is already integrated with the platforms that own those workflows. The pitch is the one ElevenLabs is uniquely positioned to make: voice as the front door for every workflow on your enterprise platform.
